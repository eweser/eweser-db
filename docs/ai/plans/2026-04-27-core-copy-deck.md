## Voice

EweserDB should sound direct, grounded, and practical — with a quiet manifesto undercurrent.

Current direction: **Solarpunk / homestead, with an individualist-sovereignty edge.** The aesthetic is built-to-last, locally-resilient, anti-extractive — closer to homesteading and the small/indie web than to Soviet propaganda. The earlier Communist-manifesto direction is retired: the iconography pointed toward collective/state ownership while the product is personal ownership, and the mismatch was confusing thoughtful readers. The solarpunk register also matches the actual audience overlap (Karpathy-wiki, local-first, r/selfhosted, IndieWeb, Fediverse-adjacent).

The defiance stays, but it's now aimed at platforms, landlords, and enclosures — not at capital. Headlines can still bite. Body copy stays calm and concrete.

The sheep finally earns its keep in this register (pastoral, regenerative, perennial). The "Ewe/you" pun is now on-theme but still discounts itself if overused. **Discipline rule: pick three or four pun moments across the entire site**, not one per section.

**Punk-sheep framing (required).** Sheep also connote following the flock, which is the exact opposite of the product's message. The mascot must be characterized as a black sheep / off-the-flock / punk sheep — visually (side-eye, pierced ear, leather, weeds in the wool) and in copy. At least one line on the landing page should rebut the herd reading: "Off the flock", "a flock of one", "the flock follows the platform — ewe don't have to." Solarpunk already carries a punk lineage; the punk-sheep is on-brand, not an outlier.

Suggested pun allocation (cap of four):

1. Footer tagline.
2. One headline-region appearance (hero kicker or a pull quote).
3. One CTA sublabel or microcopy moment.
4. One easter-egg (404, sign-in confirmation, etc.).

Beyond four, every additional pun discounts the previous ones.

Lexicon to reach for: _tend, graze, pasture, field, vault, barn, granary, plot, seed, root, graft, compost, harvest, weather, off-grid, homestead, steward, neighbor, soil, hedgerow, shelter, dry season, long winter, regrow, perennial, common._

Verbs that replace "seize": _tend, plant, root, fence, gather, keep, host, harbor, shelter, claim, hold._

Enemy framing: **landlords, extractors, platforms, monocultures, walled gardens, enclosures, the cloud-as-feudal-lord** — not "the bourgeoisie."

Rules:

- Headlines can be sharp and declarative — defiance is fine, just point it at platforms, not at class enemies.
- Body copy stays calm and concrete — the contrast with bold headlines is intentional.
- Use the pastoral metaphor lightly. One metaphor per section is plenty. When in doubt, state the product plainly.
- Avoid stacking metaphors ("plant a seed in the pasture and tend the field" — pick one).
- Avoid vague future-of-data language.
- Avoid the word "paradigm" and other bloated SaaS terms.
- Avoid making Ewe Note sound like the whole product.
- Use "user-owned data" as the center of gravity.
- Use "apps", "agents", "schemas", "rooms", "permissions", and "sync" when the product needs to feel real.
- Lead from stories and concrete frustrations before broad claims.
- Talk about AI as a practical use case for user-owned context, not as generic AI hype.
- Keep the tone fun and pointed — not paranoid or abrasive, not twee.
- Section markers are plain numbered headers (`01 — `, `02 — `). Drop the Soviet `§ I //` styling.

## Named Call-Out Strategy

Use named or name-adjacent products when they make a story more recognizable. Do not make the landing page a list of grievances against specific companies.

Good patterns:

- "If you've ever trusted tools like Evernote..."
- "Duolingo-style learning apps are great at streaks, less great at portable learning history."
- "Leaving a large ecosystem should not mean rebuilding your digital life piece by piece."
- "Some people want an Obsidian-scale knowledge base. Others just want simple notes, local sync, and reviewable AI access."

Avoid:

- "Evernote loses your notes."
- "Duolingo traps your data."
- "Google steals your life."
- "Obsidian is bloated."
